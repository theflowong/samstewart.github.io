Singular Points

We call $z = a$ a singular point if either $p_1(a)$ or $p_0(a)$ is not analytic at $a$. However, there are relatively "good" singular points, that we call regular singular points. Here $p_1(a)$ has a pole of order $1$ while $p_0(a)$ has a pole of order at most $2$. Finally, we call a point $z = a$ irregular if it is neither ordinary nor regular.
